How Debt Collection Works

Understanding the debt collection process and how to work with collectors.

1
Identify Default

Loan is in default after 2 months (60 days) of non-payment.

  • Check blacklist for defaulted borrowers
  • Verify all communication attempts
  • Document the default details
2
Choose Collector

Select a vetted debt collector based on your needs.

  • Consider location and specialty
  • Compare success rates and fees
  • Check ratings and reviews
3
Contact Collector

Reach out directly to the collector via phone or email.

  • Provide borrower details
  • Share loan documentation
  • Discuss terms and fees
4
Recovery Process

Collector works to recover the debt through various means.

  • Contact and negotiation with borrower
  • Legal action if necessary
  • Regular updates on progress
5
Settlement & Payment

Recovered funds are distributed according to agreement.

  • Collector takes agreed percentage
  • You receive remaining amount
  • Update loan status in ledger

Frequently Asked Questions

What fees do debt collectors charge?

Fees typically range from 15% to 40% of recovered amount, depending on complexity, age of debt, and collector. Some may charge flat fees or hourly rates for legal work.

How long does debt recovery take?

Recovery can take from 30 days to 6+ months depending on borrower cooperation, legal processes, and debt size. Most collectors provide estimated timelines.

What information should I provide to a collector?

Provide borrower contact details, loan agreement, payment history, communication records, and any collateral information. The more information, the better.
