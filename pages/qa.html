Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about M-pesewa's trusted micro-lending platform

General Questions

M-pesewa is an emergency micro-lending platform built around trusted social groups. It enables individuals within the same country and group to lend and borrow short-term consumption loans. All monetary transactions occur outside the platform via M-Pesa, Till Numbers, Paybill, or bank accounts. M-pesewa manages trust, structure, reputation, and visibility‚Äînot funds.

M-pesewa earns ONLY from lender subscriptions. Borrowers pay NO subscription fees. The platform does not handle loan funds, charge transaction fees, or earn from interest. Subscription revenue supports platform maintenance, trust verification, and community support services.

We operate in 13 African countries: Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, Rwanda, Burundi, Somalia, South Sudan, Ethiopia, DRC, Nigeria, South Africa, and Ghana. Each country has its own dashboard, currency support, and country-specific groups.

Yes! You can register as both a borrower and lender, but you must maintain separate profiles for each role. To switch roles, you need to log out of your current role and log in with the other role's credentials. Many community members participate in both sides of the platform.

After 2 months of non-payment, the loan is considered in default. The borrower receives a blacklist badge visible platform-wide and cannot borrow or join new groups. Removal from the blacklist requires full repayment (principal + interest + penalties) and admin approval. We also provide access to vetted debt collectors for recovery assistance.

For Borrowers

We support 16 specific emergency consumption categories: Transport (Fare), Data/Airtime, Cooking Gas, Food, Auto Repairs (Credo), Water Bill, Vehicle Fuel, Vehicle Repair, Medicine, Electricity Tokens, School Fees, TV Subscription, Wi-Fi/Internet, Advance Loan, M-Pesa Daily Sales Advance, and M-Pesa Working Capital Advance.

No. Lenders can only lend within their own group. No cross-group or cross-country lending is permitted. This maintains the trust-based, community-focused nature of the platform.

Each approved loan automatically generates a ledger under your profile. You manually update repayment status as borrowers pay you (off-platform). The ledger tracks amounts, dates, interest, penalties, and overdue status. Admin can assist with ledger updates if needed.

If your subscription expires, you lose access to the lending platform. Existing ledgers remain visible but cannot be updated. Borrowers cannot request new loans from you. Access is restored immediately upon subscription renewal.

Groups

Groups are created by founders (who become admins) and require minimum 5 members to activate. Joining is by invitation or referral only. You can search for groups in your country's dashboard and request an invitation.

Standard groups: 5-1,000 members. Lender of Lenders groups: 2-1,000 members. These limits ensure manageable, trust-based communities while allowing scalability.
